Mission

Making microbiome function reliable.

Microbes run the metabolic machinery of our planet and our bodies — cycling nutrients in soil, shaping greenhouse-gas emissions, and governing human health. Our mission is to develop the design principles that make microbiome metabolic function robust: predictable and controllable across the changing conditions of the real world.

Why it matters

Living solutions need ecological design

Unlike chemical drugs, microbial solutions are living systems. Their effects depend on the surrounding nutrient landscape and the resident community they enter — which is why a consortium that works in the lab so often fails in the field or the body. Closing that gap is essential for sustainable food production, climate-relevant greenhouse-gas mitigation, and the next generation of microbiome therapeutics.

We bridge two worlds that rarely meet: the ecology that explains how communities behave, and the engineering that lets us control them. By finding the few ecological variables that govern a function and tuning them deliberately, we aim to make microbiome function something we can design on purpose — not just observe.

What we strive for

Our goals

1

Robust function

Engineer microbial communities whose target metabolic function holds up across environments and perturbations — from the paddy field to the human body.

2

Generalizable principles

Develop methods that transfer across systems of different complexity, so insights in one community inform the design of another.

3

Impact for people & planet

Translate understanding into tools for sustainable agriculture, climate mitigation, and human health.

Our values

How we work

People first

Research and mentoring are social activities. We align each member's intrinsic curiosity with the lab's questions and support people in pursuing problems they genuinely care about.

Rigor with reach

We pair careful, quantitative experiments and theory with a focus on problems that matter beyond the lab bench.

Across disciplines

Ecology, microbiology, engineering, physics, and computation all have a seat at the table — and we collaborate widely.

Open & collaborative

We share methods and ideas, and we build research resources together with partners in Korea and abroad.
