Mission

Making microbiome function reliable — for people and the planet.

Microbes run the metabolic machinery of our planet and our bodies — cycling nutrients in soil, shaping greenhouse-gas emissions, and governing human health. Our mission is to develop the design principles that make microbiome metabolic function robust: predictable and controllable across the changing conditions of the real world.

Unlike chemical drugs, microbial solutions are living systems whose effects depend on the surrounding nutrients and the resident community — which is why they so often fail outside the lab. We bridge the ecology that explains how communities behave with the engineering that lets us control them, aiming for impact in sustainable agriculture, climate mitigation, and human health.

We work with care: research and mentoring are social activities, and we align each member's curiosity with the lab's questions. We value rigor, openness, and collaboration across ecology, microbiology, engineering, physics, and computation.
