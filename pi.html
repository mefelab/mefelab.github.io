Bio

Kiseok is a microbial ecologist who builds quantitative, design-oriented approaches to microbiome function. His doctoral work in the Kuehn Lab (University of Chicago, co-advised by Madhav Mani at Northwestern) showed that the response of complex soil microbiomes to environmental change can be captured by a small number of ecological parameters, published in Nature (2025). As a postdoc in the Kunjapur Lab (University of Delaware), he turned toward synthetic biology, devising live-therapeutic strategies for the vaginal microbiome. His lab brings these threads together: using ecology and modeling to find the levers of microbiome function, and synthetic biology to engineer that function so it holds up in the real world.
