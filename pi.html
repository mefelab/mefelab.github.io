Kiseok Lee 이기석

Principal Investigator · Assistant Professor (incoming), Department of Systems Biology, Yonsei University

Kiseok is a microbial ecologist who builds quantitative, design-oriented approaches to microbiome function. His doctoral work in the Kuehn Lab (University of Chicago, co-advised by Madhav Mani at Northwestern) showed that the response of complex soil microbiomes to environmental change can be captured by a small number of ecological parameters — published in Nature (2025). As a postdoc in the Kunjapur Lab (University of Delaware), he turned toward synthetic biology, devising live-therapeutic strategies for the vaginal microbiome.

The MEFE Lab brings these threads together: using ecology and modeling to find the levers of microbiome function, and synthetic biology to engineer that function so it holds up in the real world. He earned his B.S. at Seoul National University (Biology and Economics, double major) through the College of Liberal Studies.
