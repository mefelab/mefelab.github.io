Research

Ecological engineering of robust microbiome function.

Our central question is how to design robust microbiome function — metabolic function that holds up across the changing conditions of the real world. We identify the few ecological variables that govern a community's function and tune them, so a target function stays stable across environments and perturbations.

We pursue this through three connected directions: (1) understanding how microbiomes respond to environmental change, using perturbation experiments, time-series measurement, and consumer–resource modeling; (2) finding the right interacting functional units with machine learning on metagenomic data; and (3) engineering microbiome function with synthetic biology. We apply these ideas across two model systems of contrasting complexity — soil and rice-paddy greenhouse-gas cycling, and the vaginal microbiome.

Recent example: in soil nitrogen cycling (Lee et al., Nature 2025), just two parameters predicted nitrate dynamics across all soils — the kind of minimal, designable handle on function we aim to find and engineer.
